Projects

Social-Sentiment-Profiling-on-Public-Health-and-Safety-using-AI-ML

In today's rapidly growing landscape of social data, sentiment analysis has emerged as a powerful tool for organizations and researchers to gain valuable insights from online conversations on platforms like Twitter, Reddit, and Facebook. This technique involves analyzing user-generated content using Python and machine learning models to classify it as positive, negative, or neutral sentiments, providing a deeper understanding of public opinion on crucial topics such as the COVID-19 pandemic, crime, depression, and other societal issues. By leveraging libraries like Tweepy and TextBlob, sentiment analysis can be effectively performed on Twitter data, involving data collection, text preprocessing, sentiment classification, and visualization of sentiment trends around relevant hashtags. This approach offers several benefits, including public opinion monitoring, crisis management, social impact assessment, and brand reputation management, enabling organizations to make informed decisions while considering public sentiment, address concerns proactively, develop targeted interventions and policies, and enhance their communication strategies accordingly.
